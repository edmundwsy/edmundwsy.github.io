Projects

For me, the most alluring aspect of robotics is that you can start with pure mathematical theory, like differential manifolds, and end up screwing in bolts and welding circuits. You can seek out simple mathematical patterns in practical engineering and use elegant mathematical derivations to advance your robots. I love the pure creativity of this process.

对我来说，机器人最有魅力的一点是你可以从纯粹的数学理论例如微分流形一路做到拧螺丝、焊电路。你能够从工程实践中寻找简单的数学规律，并用优雅的数学推导服务于工程突破。我喜欢这种纯粹的创造的过程。

Learning Monocular Dense Depth From Events

1. Reproduced the research paper "Learning Monocular Dense Depth from Events" from scratch
2. Trained the reproduced model on DSEC dataset
3. Improved the performance by introducing SSIM loss
This is the course project of Seminar Computer Vision by Deep Learning at TU Delft.
PRM Planner

1. Implemented PRM planner and KD tree
2. Implemented A-star method for graph search
3. Implemented classic minimum-snap trajectory optimization (both optimization and closed-form)
4. Modified above optimization methods to confine hard-constraints.
This is the course project of Planning & Decision Making at TU Delft.
Adaptive Cruise Control with MPC

1. Modelled the problem by its piecewise affine (PWA) approximation
2. Transformed PWA model to Mixed Logical Dynamical (MLD) system
3. Designed an MPC controller for the MLD model, and rewrote it to MILP formulation
4. Applied both explicit and implicit MPC to find the optimal control policy
This is the course assignment of Modeling and Control of Hybrid Systems at TU Delft.
ADMM For Multi-Aircraft Coordination Problem

Implement solutions to the multi-aircraft coordination formulation problem based on dual decomposition, Nesterov method, incremental subgradient method and consensus ADMM approach. Investigated convergence of the optimization process under different parameter settings.
This is the course assignment of Networked and Distributed Control Systems at TU Delft.

FPGA-Based Remote-Controlled Car

We develope a remote-controlled car based on Xlinx EGO-1 FPGA board as a microcontroller. The car is equipped with L298N stepper motor drivers and 4 two-phase four-wire stepper motors. The FPGA board is utilized for bluetooth communication, instruction encoding and stepper motor control. User can send instructions with desired direction and speed via Bluetooth to control the car. The proposed design demonstrates the capabilities of FPGA-based systems for implementing complex control functions in robotic applications.
This is the project of a 3rd-year undergraduate course at XJTU.
